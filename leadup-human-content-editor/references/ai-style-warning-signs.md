# AI-style warning signs — what to detect and remove

Patterns that make text feel AI-generated. When you see these, rewrite.

## Punctuation tells

- **Em dashes (—) as the default connector.** AI loves them. Humans rarely.
  Replace with comma, period, or "and".
- **Triple-hyphen separators or random ellipses** for emphasis.
- **Smart quotes mixed with straight quotes** in the same passage.

## Word tells (default banned list)

- seamless, seamlessly
- empower, empowering, empowerment
- unlock, unleash
- robust
- cutting-edge, bleeding-edge, next-generation, state-of-the-art
- leverage (as a verb)
- elevate, harness, supercharge, revolutionize, revolutionary
- game-changing, game-changer, world-class, best-in-class
- holistic, synergy, synergies, paradigm, paradigm shift
- transformative, transformational
- "delve into", "dive deep"
- "navigate the complexities of"
- "in the realm of"
- "tapestry of"
- "at the forefront of"

## Sentence shape tells

- **"Not just X, but Y."** Classic AI parallel.
  ("It's not just a website, it's a growth engine.")
- **"It's not A. It's B."** Mini variant of the same trick.
- **Perfect tricolons in a row.** "Faster, smarter, better." "Bold, clear,
  effective." Used once it's fine. Used every line, it screams AI.
- **Balanced two-clause sentences for a whole paragraph.** Every sentence
  has the same beat.
- **Opening with the buzzword.** "Innovation drives everything we do."
- **Closing pep talk.** "The future is bright." "Let's embrace the
  journey." "Together, we can."

## Opener tells

- "In today's fast-paced world…"
- "In the ever-evolving landscape of…"
- "As we navigate the complexities of…"
- "It's no secret that…"
- "Gone are the days when…"
- "Imagine a world where…"

## Filler / hedge tells

- "It's worth noting that…"
- "As we all know…"
- "Needless to say…"
- "At the end of the day…"
- "When all is said and done…"

## Structural tells

- **Every bullet starts with the same verb.** ("Build…, Build…, Build…")
- **Every bullet is the same length.** Real humans write uneven bullets.
- **Perfect 3-bullet sections everywhere.** AI defaults to 3.
- **A paragraph that lists 4–6 benefits in one breath**, all equally vague.
- **Closing sentence summarising the paragraph** that was already clear.

## Content tells

- **Vague benefits** without a concrete outcome. ("Boost your business.")
- **Universally applicable claims** that could apply to any company.
- **No specific industry, audience, or use case** mentioned anywhere.
- **No real numbers, names, or examples** — just adjectives.
- **Same value proposition repeated in three different ways** in one block.
- **Mission-statement language for a feature description.**

## How to fix

For each tell:

- **Em dash** → comma, period, or "and".
- **Hype word** → say what the product actually does.
- **"Not just X but Y"** → drop one half, keep the stronger half.
- **Perfect tricolon** → break it; let one item run long.
- **Vague benefit** → replace with a concrete outcome from the source.
- **Generic opener** → start with the customer's actual problem or action.
- **Every-bullet-same-shape** → vary the openers and lengths.
- **Closing pep talk** → cut it, or replace with a clear next step (CTA).
