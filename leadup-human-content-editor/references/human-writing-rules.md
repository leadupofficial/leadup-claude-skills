# Human writing rules — LeadUp content editor

These are the rules to apply when rewriting AI or AI-flavoured content into
natural, human, business-ready copy. Every rule has a "why" so the rewrite
keeps meaning while losing the AI feel.

## Core principles

1. **Write the way a real owner speaks.** Imagine the founder explaining the
   product to a friendly customer over chai or coffee. Plain words first.
2. **One idea per sentence.** If a sentence has two "and"s, split it.
3. **Concrete beats clever.** Use specific nouns ("dental clinic", "WhatsApp
   reminders") instead of vague benefits ("boost growth").
4. **Vary rhythm.** Mix short and medium sentences. Avoid four sentences in a
   row of the same length and shape — that is the #1 AI tell.
5. **Active voice.** "We send the report" beats "the report is sent by us".
6. **Cut filler.** Drop "in order to", "at the end of the day", "it's worth
   noting that", "needless to say".
7. **Match the brand voice already on the site.** If the homepage is formal,
   stay formal. If it is casual, stay casual.

## Punctuation

- **No em dashes (—).** Replace with a comma, a full stop, or "and".
- Use commas sparingly. If a sentence needs three commas, it probably needs
  two sentences.
- Contractions ("we'll", "you're", "it's") are fine for most business copy.
  Avoid them in legal, proposal, or compliance sections.
- One exclamation mark per page maximum. Usually zero.

## Words to avoid (default banned list)

Replace these unless the brand voice explicitly uses them:

- seamless, seamlessly
- empower, empowering
- unlock, unleash
- robust
- cutting-edge, bleeding-edge, next-generation, state-of-the-art
- leverage (as a verb)
- elevate, harness, supercharge, revolutionize
- game-changing, world-class, best-in-class
- holistic, synergy, paradigm
- transformative, transformational
- "in today's fast-paced world"
- "in the ever-evolving landscape of"

Replacement direction: say what the product actually does. "Empower teams to
collaborate" → "Helps the team share updates without email chains."

## Structure

- Keep headings short. 3–7 words usually.
- Bullets should not all start with the same verb shape. Mix them.
- Paragraphs: 2–4 sentences. Long perfect paragraphs scream AI.
- Keep the original document structure (H1/H2, lists, CTAs). Do not flatten
  it into prose.

## Numbers and facts

- Never invent a number, percentage, year, or client name.
- If the source has a number, keep it exactly.
- If the source has a vague claim ("trusted by hundreds"), flag it for the
  user to confirm or cut.

## Voice by audience

- **End-user / consumer website**: warm, simple, second person ("you").
- **B2B / SaaS**: clear, specific, outcome-led. Less "you", more "your team".
- **Proposal / client doc**: formal but plain. No hype.
- **App UI microcopy**: short, action-led, no marketing tone.
- **Internal / technical**: precise, no marketing words at all.

## Indian / international English

- Default to clear international English. Indian brands selling globally
  should not use Hindi filler unless the brand already does.
- Avoid Indian-English habits that read odd to international readers:
  "do the needful", "kindly revert", "prepone".
- Keep currency symbols, GST mentions, and Indian-specific terms when they
  are part of the product (UPI, PAN, GSTIN).

## Self-check before output

- Zero em dashes (unless user asked).
- Zero banned hype words.
- At least 2 different sentence lengths per paragraph.
- Every claim is verifiable from the source.
- Original structure preserved.
- A real person could read this aloud without sounding like a robot.
