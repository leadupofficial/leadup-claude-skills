# LeadUp copy tone — how LeadUp work should sound

LeadUp builds client websites, multi-tenant SaaS, Flutter apps, and hosting
tools under `*.leadup.in`. Copy on those sites should feel like a real
business owner is talking, not a marketing agency on autopilot.

## Voice

- **Simple, trustworthy, business-specific.**
- Confident but not boastful. We say what we do; we do not oversell.
- Clear over clever. If a sentence needs to be read twice, rewrite it.
- Friendly but professional. Warm enough to feel human. Tight enough to
  feel competent.
- Honest about scope. If the product does one thing well, say one thing.

## What LeadUp copy is **not**

- Not full of "empower / unlock / seamless / robust".
- Not full of em dashes.
- Not generic Silicon Valley SaaS copy that could belong to any company.
- Not hype-heavy. No "world-class", "best-in-class", "game-changing".
- Not fake-personal ("we believe in dreams"). Specific examples beat
  emotional adjectives.

## Defaults by context

### LeadUp client website (agency-built site for a client)

- Conversion-focused but believable.
- Open with the customer's actual problem or actual outcome.
- Show specifics: locations, services, who it is for, how it works.
- CTAs are clear and singular: "Book a free site visit", "WhatsApp us",
  "See pricing", "Get a quote in 24 hours".
- Social proof: only what the client actually has. No invented testimonials.

### LeadUp SaaS product (LeadUp's own SaaS)

- Lead with the job the product does, not the category.
- Feature copy: what it does, who it is for, what changes after.
- Pricing copy: plain, no traps, no hidden words.
- Empty states and onboarding: short, helpful, no marketing tone.

### LeadUp proposal / client document

- Formal but plain. No hype. No mood-setting paragraphs.
- Concrete: scope, timeline, deliverables, pricing, what is **not**
  included, acceptance criteria.
- Never change numbers, dates, or scope wording during a humanising pass.

### LeadUp app / admin panel microcopy

- Action-led: "Save", "Add client", "Send reminder".
- Empty states: one short sentence + one action.
- Errors: what went wrong + what to do next. No blame, no jargon.
- No marketing words inside the product UI.

### LeadUp internal / technical (STATUS.md, handoff)

- Precise. No marketing tone at all.
- Short bullets. Facts only.
- Numbers, file paths, commands stay exact.

## Indian / international audience

- Most LeadUp clients are Indian businesses selling locally or globally.
- Default to clear international English.
- Keep India-specific terms when they are part of the product (UPI, GSTIN,
  PAN, RERA, GST invoice).
- Avoid Indian-English habits that read odd internationally ("do the
  needful", "kindly revert", "prepone").
- Currency symbols stay as the source uses them (₹, $, AED).

## Safety defaults for client copy

- Never invent client names, case studies, awards, certifications, ISO
  numbers, revenue, user counts, ratings, or testimonials.
- Flag claims that look unverifiable ("most trusted", "#1 in India",
  "trusted by thousands") for the user to confirm or cut.
- Do not soften refund / cancellation / legal language without flagging.
- Keep SEO keywords the user marked as required. Place them naturally.

## Quick checklist

- Would a real owner say this out loud?
- Does it name a specific audience / industry / outcome?
- Zero em dashes? Zero banned hype words?
- Original structure (headings, bullets, CTAs) preserved?
- Every claim verifiable from the source?
